I am Zefeng Li (李泽峰), a PhD student at the Research School of Astronomy & Astrophysics (RSAA), Australian National University. I am working on metallicity correlations with Mark Krumholz and Emily Wisnioski. Using two-point correlation function as a statistical tool, this work could help us understand how metals, specifically oxygen elements are transported and reallocated in galaxies. Besides, my research interests also include chemical evolution, star formation, and interstellar medium.

I graduated from the Department of Astronomy, Peking University (PKU) in 2017. I visited the University of Arizona in 2016, during which time I was interested in quasar variabilities, and participated in the observation of Beijing-Arizona Sky Survey (BASS) and high-redshift quasar selection.

Before coming to Australia, I worked as an algorithm engineer at Cloudwalk in Shanghai. That is the place where I learn enterprise-class machine learning coding for the purpose of optical character recognition.

My hometown is Taiyuan, the capital city in Shanxi and a place where noodles are fairly welcomed.
